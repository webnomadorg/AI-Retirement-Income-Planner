Build a Retirement Plan You Can Question

A practical guide to organising retirement income, testing withdrawals, and using AI to review your own plan — by Paul Hankin.

Most retirement advice gives you a single number and asks you to trust it. This book takes the opposite approach. Its core idea is simple but powerful: retirement income should be planned as a timeline, not as one average year. Your income changes. Your spending changes. Taxes shift, Medicare starts at a different age from your retirement date, Social Security may begin later, inflation quietly erodes purchasing power, and required minimum distributions arrive whether you planned for them or not.

A plan you can actually question shows all of that clearly — so you can understand it, test it, and improve it with confidence. That is exactly the philosophy the AI Retirement Income Planner is built on, and this book is the guide that explains the thinking behind every screen.

Why it pairs with the planner

The book teaches the why; the planner does the math. Each chapter maps to features you'll use — phases, the balance that carries forward, tax-bracket and ACA/IRMAA awareness, real-vs-nominal income, withdrawal strategies, and the built-in AI review. Read a chapter, then watch the idea play out live in your own numbers.

What you'll learn

  • Income is only the start. The difference between gross income and spendable income — and why the same monthly target can produce very different plans.
  • The balance that carries the plan forward. How sequence risk, withdrawal timing and growth shape every later year, and when a low (or high) ending balance is actually fine.
  • Taxes change the shape of income. Brackets, the standard deduction, Social Security taxation, Roth conversions and RMDs — viewed across phases, not one year in isolation.
  • Healthcare moves with income. The pre-Medicare ACA bridge, MAGI, premium tax credits, cost-sharing reductions, and IRMAA surcharges — and how withdrawals can quietly trip them.
  • Inflation and real income. Why nominal dollars can lie, how COLA and the UK triple lock help, and how to review income in real terms.
  • Comparing withdrawal strategies. The 4% rule, variable percentage withdrawal, guardrails, buckets, and floor-and-upside — matched to your situation.
  • Asking better questions — with AI. A responsible workflow for using the planner's AI chat to summarise, stress-test and propose changes to your plan, while you stay in control.

Educational only — not financial, tax or legal advice. Every projection is an estimate based on the assumptions you enter.
