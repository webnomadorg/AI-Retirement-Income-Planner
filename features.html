How it actually works

The AI proposes. The engine verifies. You decide.

Most "AI" tools give you answers from training data. This co‑pilot generates specific numeric proposals, then runs them through the planner's own deterministic calculation engine to produce a verified result — before you ever see it. Three automatic retry attempts find a solvent solution if the first pass fails.

  • Specific proposals, not vague advice — exact numbers you can read and approve
  • Simulated before shown — every proposal passes through the real engine first
  • You approve before anything changes — no silent edits to your plan
  • Your API key, your data — runs under your own Anthropic key; nothing stored or shared

Built‑in grading

Up to twelve checks grade your plan automatically.

After every change, evidence‑based benchmarks score your plan and tell you exactly what to improve — with a plain‑English explanation for each.

Income adequacy

Checks that projected withdrawals cover your spending target in every phase, accounting for inflation, income changes and the gradual depletion of balances over time.

Tax bracket efficiency

Flags if income is landing in a higher bracket than necessary, or if there's headroom to do Roth conversions at a lower rate before the next threshold kicks in.

ACA subsidy protection

Warns if your MAGI is on track to push you above the ACA income cliff and lose premium tax credits in the pre‑Medicare years — where the subsidy drop can be sudden and large.

IRMAA avoidance

Checks whether income is likely to trigger Medicare Part B and D surcharges, which apply with a two‑year lookback — so the financial hit arrives earlier than most people expect.

Portfolio survival

Confirms balances are projected to reach your plan end date without depleting, in both the base case and a silent bear‑market stress scenario run alongside it.

RMD compliance

From age 73, verifies that required minimum distributions from tax‑deferred accounts are reflected in withdrawals — helping you avoid the 25% IRS penalty for under‑distribution.

Income floor foundation

Assesses whether guaranteed income (Social Security, pension, annuity) covers essential spending, so portfolio withdrawals provide the top‑up rather than bearing the full load.

Stress test resilience

Runs a silent bear‑case scenario (−3% real returns, 5% inflation) alongside your main plan and checks whether the result remains solvent — without any extra steps from you.

Survivor income resilience

Models the income drop after the first spouse's death — including loss of the smaller Social Security benefit and single‑filer tax brackets — to surface "widow's tax cliff" situations before they happen.

CSR & subsidy‑cliff headroom

Flags any pre‑Medicare phase sitting just under a hard ACA threshold — the 250% FPL Silver cost‑sharing ceiling or the 400% FPL subsidy cliff — where unplanned income (dividends, interest, year‑end fund distributions) could tip your MAGI over and forfeit cost‑sharing or the whole premium subsidy.

Income goal

When you set a target monthly income on the My Goals card, this checks that every phase delivers at least that much in today’s dollars — and, if a phase falls short, points you to it with the gap to close.

Legacy goal

When you set a final‑balance goal, this checks whether the portfolio projected at the end of your plan meets the amount you want to leave behind — and shows how far short it falls if not.
